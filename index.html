nomadic mentality

Capturing the movement and diverse legacy of mixed race families in Southern Africa.

At the end of the 19th century, a group of one stream of my ancestors, pioneering mixed (European/Khoi) ancestors settled just beyond the northern border of the Cape Colony, the Orange River. Research done by Martin Legassick, tells us that it was said of them from their white contemporaries, that they had a “trek-geest”, a nomadic mentality. Even their missionary Christiaan Schroder leading their group mentioned his fear that they would sell their properties and trek further North for a freer life. Whilst the above perceived in a negative manner, the truth is, that for various reasons, in various time periods my family has a legacy of movement, movement for better opportunities, movement away from oppression, movement accross continents, forced movement through slavery. The author’s own movements because of missions and charity have strangely often lead her unknowingly, to the vary areas these ancestors trekked. This blog aims to document the stories, the processes, the daily finds, the timelines and photos and legacies of this profound journey spanning four hundred years and invites you to journey along and acquire a similair trek-geest!
