HAREM

My project grew out of the need I felt to document stories, especially the stories of people around me. At a certain point, I realized that in order to go forward as an artist, it was necessary to Illustrate the world I came from. It is obvious that while my photographs are expressions of my own personal history, they can also be taken as reflections of the life of Arab women in general. I can't think of myself as a representative of all Arab Women. My work therefore documents the experience of women around the world have had including myself. it is in this story I find my own voice, the unique voice of an artist. this is not an attempt to represent myself as the only victim, as this would deprive the multitudinous Realities I wish to express.

Traditionally, the presence of men has defined public spaces: From the streets, and to the places of work. Women, on the other hand, have been confined to private spaces, the home. Physical thresholds define cultural ones, hidden hierarchies dictate patterns of habitation. Thus, crossing a permissible, cultural threshold into prohibited "space" in the metaphorical sense, can result in literal confinement in an actual space. I want to stress that I do not intend my work simply as a critique of either culture, Arab or western. I am going further than critique to a more active, even subversive, engagement with cultural patterns, in order to get beyond stereotypes and convey my own experience as an Arab woman.
